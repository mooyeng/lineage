Personal social work

In case of death of a member of the Limbu community, the practice of constructing drinking water taps etc. in public places in the name of the deceased has been found for centuries. Even in the Thebe dynasty, almost all the families have adopted this custom. Even today, almost all the families do such social work according to their clusters. In this section, only the big projects carried out under the social work by the special people of Thebe family are mentioned.

Individuals doing social work and their activities:

  • 31 - Meherman (1901-1977 BS)
    He had completed the construction of a 125-meter-long suspension bridge with iron logs and iron suspension and wooden decking on the Kaveli River between Panchami and Misratar of the present Sinam VDC on 2 Baishakh 1961 BS. In addition to this, had built a dharamshala pavilion at the place where the Panchami Bazaar is located and around 1 acres of farmland as a Guthi was set aside for the maintrenance of Suspension bridge and dharmashala pavilion.
    According to the inscription placed on the Sinam side zip of the bridge, the maintenance of this bridge appears to have been done in 1971, 1981/1/20 and Jestha 2014 BS.

  • 30 - Baktu (1906-1979 BS)
    During his lifetime, Baktu had built an iron suspension bridge over the Kabeli River on the way to Penewa under Bunguwa of Ambeden VDC.

  • 34 - Indra Bahadur (1991 BS-)
    He had built a 40-meter-long iron suspension bridge on the Sosunga River between Hiruwaden of Thumbeden and Sinam Layangwa at his own expense.

  • 33 - Chittaraj/Sanghubote
    Iron suspension bridges were built between Ambeden and Tellok at Happukhola and between Sadhu Gufa and Tharpu Khamare on Eva river.
